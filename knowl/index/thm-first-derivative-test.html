Theorem 3.2.11. First Derivative Test.

Suppose that \((c,f(c))\) is a critical point of \(f(x)\) and that \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  1. If \(f'(x)\) switches from negative to positive around \(c\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has a local minimum at \(x=c\text{.}\)
  2. If \(f'(x)\) switches from positive to negative around \(c\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has a local maximum at \(x=c\)
  3. If \(f'(x)\) does not switch sign around \(c\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has neither a local maximum nor a local minimum at \(x=c\text{;}\) the most we can say is that it has a horizontal tangent line.

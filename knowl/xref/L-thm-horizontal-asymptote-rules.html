Theorem 1.3.5.

Let \(f(x) = \dfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\) be a rational function. Assume that \(p(x)\) has degree \(p\) and leading coefficient \(A\text{;}\) assume also that \(q(x)\) has degree \(q\) and leading coefficient \(B\text{.}\) Then,
  1. If \(p \gt q\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has horizontal asymptote(s).
  2. If \(p \lt q\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has horizontal asymptote(s). The equation(s) is/are \(y = \).
  3. If \(p = q\text{,}\) then \(f(x)\) has horizontal asymptote(s). The equation(s) is/are \(y = \).
